Hello! My name is Jason.

I'm from Columbus, OH.

I'm a future web/software developer currently studying with Thinkful's Engineering Immersion program.

Right now, I'm focused on learning and expanding my knowledge of all things development, and I'm currently working on HTML/CSS and Javascript! Soon, I'll be on to React, and by the end of the course, I will be a fully qualified full-stack developer! In addition, I am studying other languages, libraries, and tools in my free time, just to keep things fresh. I love a challenge, and learning new things is among my favorite pastimes.

Keep an eye on this page! It will be updated frequently as I continue this adventure in coding.

Why I Became a Developer

For almost ten years, I drove a semi truck for various companies. My drive and ambition brought me to the top of my field, and at 36 years old, I found myself driving for one of the top fleets in the country. I was proud of my accomplishments, but when I asked myself if this was the job I wanted to have for the rest of my life, I found that I had a lot of trouble saying yes. As far as I'd come, I knew that I was destined for more, and so I decided to pursue my life-long dream of getting in to software development.

$ mv truck_driver software_engineer|

My journey in to coding began when I reached the pinnacle of my truck driving career, and now I find myself starting at the very beginning of a new life, full of exciting potential. For almost a decade, I spent my time working alone, independently, relying only on myself and my own skills to keep myself out of trouble and finish the job on time. I am looking forward most of all to working on teams, meeting new people, making new friends, and learning everything I can about coding with other people to help me along the way. As I get older, I am starting to appreciate the idea of working on a team, and not alone, more and more.

Life Away From the Keyboard

For almost ten years, I drove a truck. I got to see many places in this country as I passed through them, but never stayed in one place for long. Though the hours were grueling and the work often tedious and sometimes dangerous, the lifestyle suited me because I'm always ready and eager to face the next challenge. From the careful precision and control of maneuvering an 80,000 pound big-rig down a steep hill, to the timing and coordination necessary to navigate tight docks and unyielding parking lots, I always felt challenged.

Today, I'm facing new challenges, but applying the same skills and attributes I picked up behind the wheel to them every day. Precision, adaptability, perseverance, level-headedness, patience, and problem-solving. I spent almost ten years helping to ensure that the products you know and love made it to your store shelves. And now, I want to spend my time making sure the software you use every day makes it in to your hands, ready to use or implement.

My Hobbies

Aside from coding and learning, I have a lot of passions that I pursue in my free time!

I'm a certified Advanced Open Water scuba diver, and even though it's been a while since I've been in the water, I enjoy it whenever I get to dive. When I can, I like to ride horses and camp, and I've even been whitewater rafting! I enjoy the outdoors and I'm most relaxed when I'm surrounded by trees, mountains, or water.

I am also an avid gamer. From PC and console video games, to tabletop board games, and Dungeons and Dragons, I enjoy the camaraderie that comes with joining your friends in cooperative or competitive battle. Not to mention, the skills I've picked up as a Dungeons and Dragons Dungeonmaster look great on a resume!

Lastly, I am passionate about craft beer. Especially local beers brewed here in Ohio! I enjoy all varieties of beer, and I can often be found visiting local breweries and biergartens, or looking through the local shops for my favorite brews. I am especially fond of stouts!
